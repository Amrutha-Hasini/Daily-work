About Krishna Janmashtami

Krishna Janmashtami marks the birth of Lord Krishna, the eighth avatar of Vishnu. Devotees celebrate with fasting, devotional songs, dance dramas, and midnight prayers.

The Birth of Krishna

Born in Mathura to Devaki and Vasudeva, Krishna's birth symbolizes the victory of good over evil. He was carried to safety in Gokul to be raised by Yashoda.

Traditions

People decorate temples, enact ‘Krishna Leela’ plays, and break the Dahi Handi pot. Fasting until midnight is common among devotees.

Festive Spirit

From devotional songs to the sound of flutes, the festival is filled with joy. Sweets like Makhan, Peda, and Panjiri honor Krishna’s love for butter and milk.
